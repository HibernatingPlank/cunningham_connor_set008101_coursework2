About Caesar Cipher

A Caesar Cipher also known as a shift cipher is one of the simplest ciphers and most used. This cipher uses plane text and its replaced by a fixed number down the alphabet

Example:

 Plain:    ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

The text above the normal alphabet

Cipher:   XYZABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVW

The text above is the same alphabet it has been shifted left 3 times, or right 23 times.

When you encrypt the text, the person needs to write down every letter of the message in plane line and then writes down the corresponding letter in the cipher line.

Plaintext:  THE QUICK BROWN FOX JUMPS OVER THE LAZY DOG

Ciphertext: QEB NRFZH YOLTK CLU GRJMP LSBO QEB IXWV ALD

When is the exact same but you do it in revers using the shifted number but in the other direction.
